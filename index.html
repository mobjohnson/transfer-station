Portfolio



Partify

Spreading democracy one party at a time.

Partify is an application for collaborative playlists and playback. Partify was developed as a group Rails project for the April 2014 Web Development Immersive Course at General Assembly DC. It was developed over a five day sprint to model the agile software development process including Git version control and daily SCRUMs.

Users can request songs via their mobile devices and upvote submissions.

Reel.ly

What's your top 10?

Reel.ly is an application for ranking your top 10 movies via head to head matchups, simultaneously constructing a watchlist of movies you haven't seen. Reel.ly was developed as the third and final Rails individual project for the April 2014 Web Development Immersive course at General Assembly DC over a five day sprint.

Future improvements include an improved algorithm for both presenting and sorting movie choices.

Live App » GitHub »



CafeMapper

Want to grab coffee?

CafeMapper is an application for locating nearby coffee shops based on both current location and optional user input. It utilizes the Yelp and Google Geocoder/Maps API to return cafe results and place markers on the map.

The app was built over the course of one day with an emphasis on Apple-esque aesthetic and design, including Helvetica Neue Light, circular images and opacity.

About Me

Former client services member at DC start-up newBrandAnalytics where my responsibilities included implementing new enterprise clients and managing the support desk. I contributed to the successful launch and support of clients including Five Guys, Hyatt, and the Government of the District of Columbia. In addition, I triaged, delegated and resolved up to 20 support tickets per day.

Computing and technology were always part of my lifeblood, spanning back to my computer building/tinkering days as a child. Daily interaction with the engineering and product teams at nBA continually reminded me of the ownership and possiblities of software/web development.

A crossroads was reached. Learning on nights and weekends wasn't going to cut it anymore to get where I wanted to be. In April of 2014, I took the plunge and enrolled in the Web Development Immersive at General Assembly in DC. Twelve weeks. All day. Every day. It's been one of the most rewarding experiences of my life.

Now I'm ready to contribute back to the web development community while continuing to level up my skills. The sky's the limit and I can't wait to see what's next. Drop me a line, check out my resume, or just stick around and take a look at my current/past projects. Thanks!
